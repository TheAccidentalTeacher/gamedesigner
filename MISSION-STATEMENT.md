## üõ°Ô∏è FIVE NON-NEGOTIABLES

These principles are immutable. Any feature, business decision, or partnership must align with these:

### 1. Meet Kids Where They Are
**What it means:**
- Curriculum adapts to student's actual level, not their age/grade
- Struggling students get micro-steps and extra scaffolding
- Gifted students get challenges and minimal hand-holding
- Special needs (ADHD, dyslexia, ESL) get appropriate accommodations

**What it's NOT:**
- One-size-fits-all curriculum
- "Age-appropriate" standards that ignore reality
- Rigid pacing guides that shame struggling learners

**Example:** A 5th grader reading at 2nd grade level gets 2nd grade content, not watered-down 5th grade. No shame, just progress.

---

### 2. AI-Enhanced Personalization
**What it means:**
- Claude Sonnet 4.5 (or successor) powers all tutoring
- Dynamic prompt templates merge student profile + subject + worldview
- Real-time adaptation based on student responses
- Parent coaching mode teaches parents HOW to teach

**What it's NOT:**
- Generic ChatGPT wrapper with no customization
- Automated grading with canned responses
- Surveillance AI that reports to districts

**Example:** Emily (Grade 3, struggling, YEC worldview) gets different math explanations than Jake (Grade 3, gifted, secular worldview) - same topic, personalized approach.

---

### 3. Gamified Learning
**What it means:**
- Learning should be irresistible, not insufferable
- Badges, streaks, celebrations for progress
- Scott's MAPS project approach (meaningful engagement)
- Fun without sacrificing rigor

**What it's NOT:**
- Candy Crush-style distractions
- Points for busywork
- Shallow "edutainment" with no substance

**Example:** Student completes 7 days of math practice ‚Üí "Great Commendation" badge + encouraging message + mastery visualization.

---

### 4. Cut the Bloat
**What it means:**
- Give families what they WANT, nothing they don't
- No forced subjects (if you don't want art, don't pay for art)
- No 300-page teacher's manuals
- No vendor lock-in or upsell tricks

**What it's NOT:**
- $2,000 curriculum packages with unused books
- Subscription bundles that include junk
- "Comprehensive" programs that overwhelm parents

**Example:** Parent wants Math + Reading only ‚Üí Pays for Math + Reading only. No forced bundles. No hidden fees.

---

### 5. Relationship-Focused
**What it means:**
- "My life is better because you're in it" philosophy
- AI tutors celebrate effort, not just results
- Parent coaching is empathetic, not condescending
- Community features connect families

**What it's NOT:**
- Cold, clinical "learning management system"
- Behavior tracking and surveillance
- Shaming language for mistakes

**Example:** Student struggles with fractions for 3rd time ‚Üí AI says: "Fractions are tough! Let's break this down together. I'm proud of you for sticking with it."

## üîß TECHNICAL PRINCIPLES

- **Backend**: Node.js + Express (NOT Python/Django)

### Architecture Principles
- **Simplicity**: No microservices until absolutely necessary
- **Modularity**: Components can be extracted and reused
- **Self-hosted**: No cloud vendor lock-in where possible
- **Open Standards**: REST API, PostgreSQL, JSON (no proprietary formats)

### Scott's Teaching Philosophy
1. **Sit on the floor with crying kids** (incarnational education)
2. **"My life is better because you're in it"** (relationship first)
3. **Meet kids where they are** (not where standards say)
4. **Celebrate effort, not just results** (growth mindset)
5. **Make learning irresistible** (gamification with purpose)

### Prompt Template Strategy
**48 Templates** (immutable categories):
- Base (3): student-tutor, parent-coach, prerequisite-check
- Teaching Modes (5): struggling, gifted, ADHD, dyslexia, ESL
- Worldviews (3): YEC, OEC, Secular
- Subject-Specific (34): Math, Reading, Science, History, Languages, etc.
- Special (3): grammar, celebration, standards

**How Templates Work**:
1. System loads base template
2. Adds teaching mode based on student performance
3. Adds worldview based on family preference
4. Adds subject-specific teaching strategies
5. Injects variables (name, grade, topic)
6. Sends ~8,000 token prompt to Claude

**Result**: Every student gets personalized AI tutor that:
- Speaks at their grade level
- Adapts to their learning style
- Respects their family's worldview
- Uses proven teaching strategies for that subject
